Cockroach Janata Party

A political front of the youth, by the youth, for the youth:
Secular · Socialist · Democratic · Lazy

Origin

On 15 May 2026, Chief Justice Surya Kant described unemployed young Indians as "cockroaches" and "parasites of society" from the bench of the Supreme Court of India.

The next day, Abhijeet Dipke — a political communications strategist and former Aam Aadmi Party campaigner — founded the Cockroach Janata Party. We took the name. We wore it.

"We are not here to set up another PM CARES, holiday in Davos on the taxpayer's salary slip, or rebrand corruption as 'strategic spending.'"

Who We Are

A movement with no interest in becoming a traditional political party or recruiting established politicians. We build a platform for young people who keep getting called lazy, chronically online, and — most recently — cockroaches.

Religion, caste, and gender play no role in membership. If you are young and angry and honest, you are already one of us.

Five-Point Manifesto

  1. 1. Judicial Independence No Chief Justice shall receive a Rajya Sabha seat or any post-retirement government appointment as reward. Judges serve the Constitution, not the ruling coalition.
  2. 2. Election Integrity Deleting legitimate votes is an act of terrorism against democracy. Activists arrested under UAPA for exposing vote suppression are political prisoners, not criminals. The Chief Election Commissioner must be held personally accountable for every suppressed vote.
  3. 3. Women's Representation 50% of all Cabinet positions and parliamentary seats must be held by women. Tokenism is not representation.
  4. 4. Media Freedom Cancel the broadcast licences of Adani-owned and Reliance-owned media outlets. Investigate the financial accounts of "Godi media" anchors who function as government press offices.
  5. 5. Anti-Defection Any politician who switches party affiliation faces a mandatory 20-year ban from contesting elections. Democracy is not a revolving door.

Membership Criteria

You qualify if you are:

  • Unemployed — by force, by choice, or by principle
  • Lazy — physical activity only
  • Chronically online — minimum 11 hours per day
  • Capable of ranting with sharp, honest content

Wealth, religion, caste, gender, and formal credentials are not considered.
